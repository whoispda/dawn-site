Why I built Dawn

I became a parent, and time fell apart. Was that feed two hours ago, or four? Did the baby nap? At 3am I couldn't have told you my own name.

I'm one developer. Every app I tried wanted an account, an email, a subscription. Then it wanted to sell what I typed in about my kid. All I wanted was to remember the last feed without a fight.

So I built the app I needed at 3am. Dawn logs in a tap. It learns your baby's own rhythm and tells you what's next. And it runs entirely on your phone. No account, nothing uploaded, ever.

The smart stuff other apps lock behind a paywall? Free. A tired parent shouldn't have to pay to understand their own baby.

I'm building this in the cracks between feeds, in the open. If it makes one exhausted parent's night a little clearer, that's the whole point.

— FounderSolo developer · first-time parent · maker of Dawn

Good to know

Questions parents ask

Is Dawn really free?

Yes. The tracking and the on-device predictions other apps put behind a subscription are free. One optional upgrade, Nap Labs, adds depth like to-the-minute nap timing. The core stays free, forever.

Where does my data go?

Onto your device, and if you use iCloud, into your own private iCloud account for backup and sync. It never reaches us. We have no server and collect nothing. Really.

Does it work for two parents?

Your data syncs across your own devices through private iCloud, so your phones stay in step. Shared access between separate accounts is on the way.

Is this medical advice?

No. Dawn records and visualizes what you log and shows patterns from your own data. It isn't a diagnostic tool. Always check with your pediatrician.

What does it run on?

iPhone, with a Home Screen widget, Lock Screen Live Activities, and Siri. Join the list below and we'll tell you the moment it's live.
